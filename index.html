How AI Shapes Our World: Physical Landscapes of AI

Introduction

This project is a multimedia approach to explore how AI transforms physical environments through resource consumption and AI infrastructure used to keep artificial intelligence and other technology running. Specifically, my project focuses on the data centers that power those systems. Inspired by readings such as Kate Crawfords "Anatomy of an AI," Isaac Asimov's "The Last Question," Dario Amodei's "Machines of Loving Grace," and world-building works I've came across in other classes such as Ian Chengs' "Emissaries." I explored various ideas of what our future could look like as a result of AI, through conversations with it. This project was guided by Claude for prompt generation that I used to make images in ChatGBT Pro, as well as Eleven Labs for audio. I also used Claude to point me in the direction of many of the resources that served as inspiration for the project, as well as some research, such as the MIT report titled, "Explained: Generative AI's Environmental Impact" and a Bloomberg article by Leonardo Nicoletti, Michelle Ma, and Dina Bass titled, "AI is Draining Water From Areas that Need it Most." Those sources, along with others listed in my bibliography, were used to guide the direction of the project and Claude's prompt generation.

Using an AI LLM, image generator, and audio generator, highlights the intensive use of the tools while examining their environmental footprint. To converse with Claude, make each image, and create each audio took many prompts by myself to get a result I was happy with. There was an irony in using these tools to point out the impact of data centers. Each image and audio pair focuses on a different aspect of data center impact: rural landscape transformation, power grid adaptation, water depletion, and thermal emissions. The audio components immerse the viewer in a world where those impacts only have gotten worse. This translates resource consumption into a sensory experience.

Overall, this project serves as both technological critique and environmental awareness. By making visible what is normally hidden from AI users, these visuals reflect on how our digital devices and tools that make our lives easier, are actually causing a global unpaid workforce and mass loss of natural resources from the infrastructures that extract them. The research questions guiding this project are:

  • How might AI reshape our physical environment and daily-life in the near or distant future?
  • What could generated visions for the future reveal about current worries or hopes for AI's development?
  • How might our physical environment change if these predictions come true? What would cities look like? How would people interact with their environment?

Rural Transformation

Image Prompt

Wide-angle landscape of a rural farming area being transformed by new data center construction. Early morning light with mist over fields. Massive warehouse-like data center structure partially completed, with cooling infrastructure visible. New power lines and electrical substations crossing previously undisturbed farmland. Clear visual contrast between agricultural patterns and geometric industrial development. Construction vehicles creating access roads. Some fields appear drier near the development while others remain fertile at a distance. Natural landscape elements are still dominant but showing clear signs of technological intrusion. No people visible, cinematic composition with atmospheric depth, highly detailed rendering, professional landscape photography style, dramatic lighting highlighting both natural and technological elements.

Audio Prompt

Create an audio transition that begins with peaceful rural morning sounds (birds, light wind in crops, distant tractor) that gradually transforms into construction sounds (excavator, earth moving) and finally settles into the constant electrical hum and cooling systems of a data center. The transition should feel ominous and convey environmental loss.

Reflection

"Rural Transformation" depicts the impact of data center infrastructure into agricultural landscapes. As illustrated in Kate Crawfords "Atlas of an AI," these structures represent how that kind of materialization from cloud computing can transform once productive farmlands into industrial compounds. Many of the lands used are ones that have already been damaged greatly by environmental impacts. The excavator machine in the foreground reflects the continuation of making use of the land for more industrialization, changing habitat and reducing resources in the process. This reflects how even to create the AI's there's much environmental damage, and it continues when they are used and run through these centers. The audio of the bird chirping and fading out depicts how this process takes over the land of wildlife in these spaces as well. This is similar to Crawfords concept of AI's "hidden material cost." We aren't realizing how quickly digital infrastructures are replacing agricultural systems, revealing the true impact of our digital usage of AI.

Power Grid Adaptation

Image Prompt

Aerial view of a regional power grid being expanded to support multiple data centers. Massive new transmission lines converging on industrial complexes housing servers. Large electrical substations with transformers and switching equipment. Backup generator farms with diesel tanks adjacent to the main facilities. Visual storytelling through infrastructure scale - showing energy hunger through density of power equipment. Some solar arrays are visible but dwarfed by traditional power infrastructure needs. Golden hour lighting creates dramatic shadows of power lines across the landscape. Technical illustration quality with photorealistic rendering, showing the relationship between energy production, transmission, and consumption by data centers. Highly detailed image with particular attention to electrical infrastructure elements.

Audio Prompt

Create an audio landscape of electrical infrastructure with high-voltage transmission sounds, transformer hums, and electrical substations. Include rhythmic pulses that suggest power consumption patterns, growing more intense over time. The overall feeling should be industrial, vast, and slightly threatening.

Reflection

"Power Grid Adaptation" is a depiction of the massive energy infrastructure that supports data centers. The transmission towers and electrical substations shown in the image represent AI's "staggering" electricity consumption, according to the MIT article about generative AI's environmental impact. Power grids in this capacity cause negative environmental concerns such as electromagnetic fields that affect wildlife by causing habitat fragmentation, and an abundance of fossil fuels to meet generation needs. The audio paired with this image is meant to represent the pattern of energy flow through these data centers that is based on actual consumption cycles. As mentioned in "A Geology of Media," by Jussi Parikka, these energy networks are "infrastructures of surveillance capitalism" that take data demands directly into the atmosphere and landscape as a result of resource extraction and emissions.

Water Resource Depletion

Image Prompt

Photorealistic aerial view of a massive data center complex depleting local water resources in a drought-prone region. Central focus on enormous water intake infrastructure drawing from a visibly diminished reservoir or river. Stark contrast between the artificially maintained green lawns and cooling ponds of the data center versus the parched, cracked earth of surrounding agricultural land. Multiple cooling towers releasing water vapor while nearby farms show withered crops. Pipeline infrastructure cutting across the landscape from water source to facility. Visual evidence of falling water table: exposed shorelines, dry wells, abandoned irrigation equipment in nearby fields. Local communities with empty water towers in the distance while the data center maintains filled retention ponds. Heat shimmer visible above the facility. Dramatic drought-stricken landscape with dramatic lighting highlighting the inequity of water distribution.

Audio Prompt

Create a soundscape that contrasts flowing water sounds (river, rainfall) with emphasis on industrial water usage (pumps, mechanical processing) and the sounds of drought (dry wind, cracking earth). Begin with natural water sounds that gradually diminish as industrial water usage intensifies, ending with an emphasis on drought sounds. The emotional quality should shift from peaceful to mechanical to concerning.

Reflection

"Water Resource Depletion" shows how data centers in this future redirect water resources for cooling needs. This is often done in regions that are already vulnerable to drought. The landscape is dry and cracking, showing the physical strain on the land to collect water for the purpose of running data centers that make AI possible. One of the most common arguments against AI is that each prompt uses about 16 ounces of water. This is explored in the Bloomberg report on water-hungry data centers. The report from Bloomberg documents the large amount of states consuming millions of gallons of water daily (five states alone account for 72% of new data centers built in high stress areas), causing surrounding communities/ecosystems to face water-scarcity. The image made by ChatGBT depicts a contrast between thriving technological abundance that these facilities create but also the environmental deprivation. The audio paired with the image transitions from flowing water to an overlap of mechanical sounds, redirecting viewers from the resource being used to who is using it. This image reflects how AI's unnoticed "physical" needs often are the priority compared to preservation of our communities and agriculture. Like the image, this would drive people away from these areas due to resource inequalities. Crawford refers to this exchange of digital needs and data growth over environmental health as "sacrifice zones." The image depicts that well, as not much is left in the vast landscape.

Thermal Impact Zone

Image Prompt

Photorealistic visualization of the extensive thermal impact zone surrounding a major data center complex. Infrared-style imaging showing heat signature spreading outward from the facility in concentric patterns. Clear ecological zonation visible: heat-tolerant invasive species thriving near the center, stunted native vegetation at middle distances, and healthier ecosystems only far from the facility. Microclimate effects visible with localized cloud formation directly above heat sources and altered precipitation patterns. Snow melted in unnatural patterns around the complex while remaining on distant landscapes. Heat shimmer distorting the air above cooling systems releasing waste heat. Thermal plumes visible from water discharged into local waterways, affecting aquatic ecosystems. Nighttime imaging showing how the heat island prevents normal cooling, disrupting wildlife patterns.

Audio Prompt

Create an audio representation of heat being generated and dissipated from a data center into its environment. Include low-frequency drones representing waste heat, server processing sounds, cooling systems under strain, and the subtle sounds of environmental alteration (ice melting, steam rising). The sound should build in intensity and create a sense of constant, relentless thermal output that never ceases.

Reflection

"Thermal Impact Zone" uses complementary colors and contrast to emphasize the heat generation caused by data centers. According to MIT's report, this environmental impact of AI infrastructure is often overlooked. The steam released is visible, but the overall environmental temperature increase is not. According to the analysis of satellite thermal imaging from Bloomberg, data centers create recognizable "heat signatures" that can be detected from space. This emphasises the global impact of heat islands, as shown in the orange areas of the image, that environmental scientists call "infrastructure-induced climate patches." that disrupt weather patterns (Bloomberg). The heat islands also create localized climate effects such as disrupting vegetation patterns, changing wildlife behavior, and precipitation. The image highlights this through the melting snow outside the data center. The paired audio represents the constant and concerning buzz of layered frequencies coming from the data center. This allows for a more realistic immersion into the climate vulnerabilities the heat emissions cause, and the external costs.

Conclusion

Overall, this project uses AI tools to examine AI's environmental impact to create somewhat ironic commentary on these systems. That irony serves as a vital part of the research and process, as bias's of the AI become present before enough prompting is done. For example, earlier audio samples sounded too hopeful, such as a mostly quiet soundscape with birds chirping. Using AI to examine itself also, of course, uses the data center processing power discussed. But doing so highlights how these systems can work as both the subject and medium of critique, awareness, and understanding. As explored in class, AI requires understanding beyond its anatomy and coding. Developing a better understanding of AI through research and hands-on experience allows for better examination of the cultural, environmental, and ethical contexts and concerns that the technology and data centers present. The environmental impacts of AI is just one aspect of many other possible impact explorations, but connects digital tools to physical consequences in the most direct way. This project allows for more transparency about AI tools and their cost. Acknowledging the cost and understanding them allows for more informed decisions to be made about our future with advanced technology and our own environment.
